# BEAM Transportation — Video Production Script
## Midjourney + Veo3/Flow Production Guide
### Version 1.0 · ReadyAimGo Internal

---

## OVERVIEW

**Runtime:** 75–90 seconds  
**Tone:** Grounded, purposeful, Milwaukee-proud. Not a hype reel. Not a charity video. Something in between — professional infrastructure meets community investment.  
**Audience (primary):** VC414, Balboa Capital, Jordan's network, early investors  
**Audience (secondary):** BEAM cohort recruits, UWM/MATC students, RAG clients  
**Music direction:** Sparse, cinematic. Think Burial meets Jon Hopkins. Low percussion, space, no lyrics. Builds slowly through the middle and opens up at the end.  
**Color palette:** Dark backgrounds, amber/orange accents, clean white UI. Matches the fleet browser and cohort page aesthetic.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Midjourney tips for this project
- Use `--style raw` to suppress Midjourney's tendency toward illustration. You want photorealism throughout.
- For the vehicle shots: if you have real photos of the LYRIQ or Transit, use Midjourney's image-to-image feature (drag in photo + prompt) to maintain consistency.
- The person in Shot 3B should look like Milwaukee — not a stock photo. Use `authentic Milwaukee neighborhood` in the prompt and avoid `professional headshot` language.
- Generate 4 variations of each shot and pick the one that feels most like a real frame from a documentary, not an ad.

### Veo3/Flow tips for this project
- Keep motion slow throughout Segments 1–2. Speed up slightly in Segment 3 (the loop moves with purpose). Slow back down for 4 and 5.
- Use Flow's transition feature between 3A → 3B → 3C → 3D → 3E. These should feel like one continuous sequence, not five separate cuts.
- For the map shot (2C), generate the base map in Midjourney, then use Veo3 to animate the dots and lines. Don't try to do both in one prompt.
- The end card (5B) should be generated in your video editor (CapCut, Premiere, DaVinci), not in Veo3. Type animations are cleaner handled outside the AI.

### Music licensing
- Options: Artlist.io (recommended), Musicbed, or Epidemic Sound
- Search terms: `cinematic minimal dark ambient` `industrial contemplative` `purposeful slow build`
- Avoid anything with a big emotional swell — this video earns its emotion through the work, not the score.

### Voiceover recording
- Record in a quiet room with a decent USB mic (Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB, or similar)
- Speak slower than feels natural — on video, slow reads as confident
- Record 3 full takes. Pick the most grounded one, not the most energetic
- Light compression and a small amount of reverb in post (not the recording environment)

## PLACEMENT STRATEGY

| Location | Page | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **1st priority** | `readyaimgo.biz/fleet` | Fleet clients seeing the vehicle browser should see the video first — converts browsers to buyers |
| **2nd priority** | `transportation.beamthinktank.space` homepage | Cohort recruits need to see the mission before they scroll to roles |
| **3rd priority** | VC414 pitch deck / one-pager | Send as a Loom or YouTube unlisted link in the intro email to Jordan's contact |
| **4th priority** | `readyaimgo.biz/story` | Supports the existing narrative section |
| **5th priority** | LinkedIn (personal + RAG company page) | Public launch — post when first vehicle is acquired and first cohort session is complete |

**Do not post publicly until:**
- The first vehicle (LYRIQ) is acquired
- At least one cohort session has been logged
- The `/fleet` page and `/cohort` page are both live and populated with real data

That way every link in the video works and every claim in the narration is verifiable.
